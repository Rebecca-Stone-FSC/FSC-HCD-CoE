PROJECT OVERVIEW
================================================================================
This service blueprint documents the complete intake process for the VA 
Financial Services Center (FSC) AI Center of Excellence (CoE). It visualizes
the journey from initial idea submission through final approval and handoff, spanning approximately 6 weeks.

The blueprint serves as a comprehensive reference for:
- Idea submitters (anyone in the organization)
- AI CoE team members
- FTS Intake Team
- Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)
- Leadership and decision-makers

BLUEPRINT STRUCTURE
================================================================================

9 PROCESS PHASES:
1. Ideation (Day 0)
   - Initial idea generation and problem identification
   
2. Pre-Intake [Optional] (Week 1-2)
   - Optional workshops and collaborative ideation sessions
   
3. Submission (Day 1)
   - Formal idea submission via SharePoint or email
   
4. Review (Week 1)
   - Initial triage and feedback from AI CoE team
   - 1-2 day response time
   
5. Business Case (Week 2-3)
   - Development of ROI, metrics, and business value
   - Collaborative work between submitter and AI CoE
   
6. Operations Case (Week 3-4)-Needs verification
   - Process mapping and operational impact assessment
   
7. Technical Case (Week 4-5)-Needs verification
   - Technical feasibility and architecture review
   
8. Approval (Week 6)-Needs verification
   - Final presentation and committee decision
   
9. Handoff (Week 6+)-Needs verification
   - Transition to FTS Intake or delivery team

5 SWIMLANES:
1. Digital Touchpoints (Purple)
   - Online forms, portals, documents, and digital tools
   
2. Submitter Actions (Blue)
   - Activities performed by the idea submitter
   
3. Frontstage - Visible (Pink)
   - AI CoE team activities visible to the submitter
   
4. Backstage - Invisible (Teal)
   - Internal AI CoE processes hidden from submitter
   
5. Support Systems (Gold)
   - Underlying tools, databases, and infrastructure

3 SERVICE BLUEPRINT LINES:
1. Line of Interaction
   - Separates submitter actions from frontstage activities
   
2. Line of Visibility
   - Separates frontstage (visible) from backstage (invisible)
   
3. Line of Internal Interaction
   - Separates backstage activities from support systems

STAKEHOLDER ROLES
================================================================================

IDEA SUBMITTERS:
- Anyone in the organization with an idea
- No technical expertise required
- Supported throughout the process

AI COE TEAM:
- Workshop facilitators (pre-intake phase)
- Intake coordinators (initial triage)
- Business analysts (ROI and metrics)
- Operations specialists (process mapping)
- Solution architects (technical assessment)
- Collaboration approach (not gatekeeping)

FTS INTAKE TEAM:
- Receives approved use cases for implementation
- Manages charge codes and project tracking
- Coordinates delivery resources

LEADERSHIP:
- Innovation Committee (final approval)
- Executive sponsors (business support)
- Finance team (budget approval)


KNOWN PAIN POINTS & OPPORTUNITIES
================================================================================

PAIN POINTS (Identified through limited user research):
- Submitters often confuse AI with automation
- Perfectionism paralysis during business case development
- Frequent delays due to missing LTTE charge codes
- Lack of proper business sponsorship in some cases
- Unclear collaboration phase expectations

OPPORTUNITIES (Improvement areas):
- Streamline pre-submission education resources
- Create charge code checklist early in process
- Develop clearer collaboration phase deliverables
- Build library of example business cases
- Enhance communication touchpoints throughout journey
